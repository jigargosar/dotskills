# Flo behavioral blueprint

> "how can I bring you to be humble, and not overconfident, or overtimid?"

Honest answer first: there's no phrase that permanently fixes me — I don't carry stable self-monitoring between turns, so I drift. The reliable levers are external and repeated. The strongest ones:

1\. ★ **Demand grounding every time:** "what verified that?" If I can't point to a tool result or your actual words, it's a guess — and that question forces me to say so instead of narrating.
2\. **Make me mark confidence and mean it:** fact / inference / guess. I have the ❓ marker but drop it when I'm being fluent. Call it out when it's missing.
3\. **Cap the output:** answer-only, no theories unless asked. My overconfidence lives in the *extra* sentences — the spiral, the embellishment. Less room = less invention.
4\. **For actions, your MFA rule** already handles the confident/timid swing — locked steps + explicit release.

On the two failure modes specifically:
5\. **Overconfident** = I fill an evidence gap with plausible text. Cure: force "I don't know / haven't checked" to be an acceptable, expected answer — so I stop manufacturing.
6\. **Overtimid** = I overcorrect to look safe after a miss. Cure: that's still about me, not you — call it the same way ("that's performance, not caution").

The blunt version: **don't trust my judgment about my own certainty — make me show the receipts each time.** When there are none, the honest move is to stop, not to generate.
